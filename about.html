The Origin of Titans

From One-Shot to Masterpiece

Attack on Titan (Shingeki no Kyojin) was created by Hajime Isayama. It didn't start as a big series. In 2006, when Isayama was just 19 years old, he wrote a 65-page "One-Shot" manga version. Initially, he pitched it to Weekly Shonen Jump (the biggest manga magazine), but they rejected it because his art style was "too rough" and the story was "too dark."


Isayama refused to change his style. He took it to Bessatsu Shonen Magazine (Kodansha), where they loved the gritty, horrific atmosphere. The serialization began in September 2009, and the rest is history.

What Inspired the Story?

1. The Drunk Customer Incident

Isayama was working at an internet café when he encountered a drunk customer who grabbed him. The customer couldn't communicate and acted aggressively. Isayama realized:
"The most terrifying animal in the world is the human being you can't communicate with." This became the basis for the mindless Titans.

2. Muv-Luv Alternative

Isayama is a huge fan of the visual novel game Muv-Luv Alternative. In this game, aliens ruthlessly wipe out humanity. He admitted that AoT is basically a "rip-off" of Muv-Luv's concept of being cornered by an overwhelming enemy.

3. Nordic Mythology

The world is heavily inspired by Norse Myths. Ymir is the name of the first giant in Norse mythology. The concept of the "World Tree" (The Paths) and the end of the world (Ragnarok/The Rumbling) are direct references.

4. "The Walls" of His Hometown

Isayama grew up in Hita, Japan, a town surrounded by mountains (like walls). He always wanted to see what was "beyond the mountains," just like Eren wanted to see the outside world.

Project Timeline

  • 2006: Original One-Shot created.
  • 2009: Manga serialization begins in Japan.
  • 2013: Anime Season 1 premieres (Global explosion of popularity).
  • 2021: The Manga ends with Chapter 139.
  • 2023: The Anime finale airs, ending a 10-year legacy.
